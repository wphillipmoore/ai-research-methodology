# Product Name Proposals: AI Research Methodology

**Date**: 2026-04-08
**Status**: Initial proposals — gathering feedback
**Context**: The AI Research Methodology needs a proper product name and CLI
command. "AI Research Methodology" is descriptive but not a product name — it's
too long, not memorable, and makes for a terrible CLI command.

## Design Criteria

- Represents the **search for truth without fear**
- Rooted in mythology, literature, philosophy, or pop culture
- Short enough for a CLI command (or has a natural abbreviation)
- Distinctive — not already overused in tech
- Strong logo potential
- Memorable

## Top Candidates

### 1. Diogenes (`dio`)

**Origin**: Diogenes of Sinope (Greek, ~412-323 BCE) — the Cynic philosopher
who walked through Athens in broad daylight carrying a lantern, "searching for
an honest man." He refused to accept anything at face value and was famous for
his fearlessness: when Alexander the Great visited him and offered to grant any
wish, Diogenes told him to move — he was blocking the sunlight.

**Why it fits**: The image of someone who refuses to accept surface-level claims
and relentlessly searches for truth is a precise match for what this tool does.
The fearlessness angle — truth-seeking without deference to authority or
convention — aligns with the product's philosophy.

**CLI**: `dio` — three characters, fast to type, distinctive.

**Logo potential**: A lantern. Instantly recognizable, strong visual identity.

### 2. Aletheia (`aletheia`)

**Origin**: Greek (Aletheia) — literally "un-concealment" or "un-forgetting."
The word breaks down as *a-* (not) + *lethe* (forgetting/concealment). In Greek
mythology, Lethe was the river of forgetting in the underworld. Aletheia is the
opposite force: the act of pulling truth out of obscurity. The Greek concept of
truth wasn't about correctness — it was about *revealing what's hidden*.

**Why it fits**: Structurally identical to what the tool does. It takes
information clouded by noise, bias, and uncertainty, and performs
un-concealment. The etymology opposes Lethe (forgetting/obscuring), which is
exactly the enemy the tool fights.

**CLI**: `aletheia` — beautiful but long (8 characters). Could abbreviate but
loses elegance.

**Logo potential**: An eye emerging from mist, a veil being pulled back.

### 3. Satya (`satya`)

**Origin**: Sanskrit for truth. Gandhi's *Satyagraha* literally translates to
"truth-force" or "holding firmly to truth." It carries the connotation of truth
as something you pursue with conviction — not something handed to you. The
concept of truth *without fear* is literally the Satyagraha ethos.

**Why it fits**: The "truth without fear" angle is the strongest here of any
candidate. Short, punchy, carries deep philosophical weight.

**CLI**: `satya` — five characters, easy to type, phonetically clear.

**Logo potential**: Could draw from Ashoka Chakra imagery or abstract
truth/light motifs.

### 4. Ma'at (`maat`)

**Origin**: Egyptian goddess of truth, justice, and cosmic order. In the
afterlife judgment, she weighed the hearts of the dead against her feather — if
your heart was heavier than truth, you were devoured. The weighing of claims
against evidence is a direct parallel.

**Why it fits**: The feather-on-a-scale metaphor maps cleanly to claim
verification. Evidence is weighed; claims that don't hold up are identified.

**CLI**: `maat` — four characters.

**Logo potential**: The feather of truth on a scale. Iconic, elegant.

### 5. Theia (`theia`)

**Origin**: Greek titaness of sight and clarity. Her name literally means
"divine vision." Mother of Helios (the sun) and Selene (the moon). She grants
the ability to see clearly.

**Why it fits**: Clarity and vision — cutting through noise to see what's
actually there. Clean, short, distinctive.

**CLI**: `theia` — five characters.

**Logo potential**: Light/vision motifs, solar imagery.

## Honorable Mentions (Considered and Set Aside)

| Name | Origin | Why it fits | Why it was set aside |
|------|--------|-------------|----------------------|
| **Veritas** | Roman goddess of truth | Direct, recognizable | Overused in tech and academia |
| **Mimir** | Norse keeper of the well of wisdom | Wisdom through sacrifice (Odin gave an eye to drink from Mimir's well) | Obscure, hard to spell for most people |
| **Prometheus** | Greek titan who stole fire for humanity | Defiant truth-bringer, light in the darkness | Heavily overused in tech (monitoring tool, movie, etc.) |
| **Lumen** | Latin for light | Illumination metaphor | Too generic, lacks distinctiveness |

## Author's Initial Preference

**Product name**: Diogenes
**CLI command**: `dio`

### Rationale

Diogenes checks every box: the philosophy is a precise match (relentless,
fearless truth-seeking), the abbreviation `dio` is short and distinctive for
CLI use, and the lantern makes for a strong visual identity.

There is also an intentionally subversive secondary reason for the choice.
Ronnie James Dio — widely regarded as one of the greatest heavy metal vocalists
in the genre's history — fronted Black Sabbath, Rainbow, and his own band Dio
across decades. He was legendary for both his extraordinary vocal power and,
ironically, for being one of the shortest frontmen in metal. The name carries a
personal connection to the author's lifelong appreciation for heavy metal, and
the dual reference adds a layer of personality and humor that makes the name
more memorable.

A product called Dio that searches for truth with a lantern — and also happens
to share a name with the man who sang "Holy Diver" — is exactly the kind of
name that sticks.
